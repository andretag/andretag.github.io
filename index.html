Andrea Tagliabue

Hi! I am Andrea, and I am currently a graduate student at the Aerospace Controls Laboratory (ACL), part of the Laboratory for Information and Decision Systems (LIDS) at MIT.

My research interests lie at the intersection of learning, sensing and control for autonomous systems. My goal is to reduce the gap between flying animals and engineered systems by developing data-driven, dynamics-informed and computationally efficient algorithms that enable agile flight under uncertainty, either due to disturbances, lack of data, or model errors. I am grateful to be funded by the AFOSR NISC MURI, where I work in collaboration with a team of biologists, nuroscientists, engineers, control theorists and machine learning experts who provide critical tools needed by my research.

Prior to MIT, I was a robotic engineer affiliate at the NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory and at Caltech in Pasadena, CA, and member of the JPL/Caltech/MIT team for the Darpa Subterranean Challenge. My work focused on state estimation and localization in subterranean environments, and I worked on a lidar-inertial odometry algorithm.

I finished a Masters in Robotics, Systems, and Control in 2018, at ETH Zürich, and I carried out my thesis at the High Peformance Robotics Lab at U.C. Berkeley, developing a novel algorithm to improve energy-efficiency of a multicopter.

During my master, I was research assistant at the Autonomous Systems Lab at ETH, where I worked on communication-less object transportation for a swarm of UAVs, and I was a member of the ETH team for the MBZ Int. Robotic Challenge.

I completed my Bachelor at Politecnico di Milano in 2015 studying Automation Engineering (EECS).
