Andrea Tagliabue

Hi! I am Andrea, and I am currently a robotic engineer at the NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory and at Caltech in Pasadena, CA. I am also a member of the JPL/Caltech/MIT team for the Darpa subterranean challenge. My work focuses on state estimation and localization in subterranean environments, and I am currently working on a lidar-inertial odometry algorithm for rotary-wing UAVs.

I finished a Masters in Robotics, Systems, and Control in 2018, at ETH Zürich, and I carried out my thesis at the High Peformance Robotics Lab at U.C. Berkeley, developing a novel algorithm to improve energy-efficiency of a multicopter.

During my master, I was research assistant at the Autonomous Systems Lab at ETH, where I worked on communication-less object transportation for a swarm of UAVs, and I was a member of the ETH team for the MBZ Int. Robotic Challenge.

I finished my Bachelor at Politecnico di Milano in 2015 studying Automation Engineering (EECS).
